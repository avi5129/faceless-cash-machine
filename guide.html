1. What this is (and what it is NOT)

This guide documents a working, fully-local pipeline that turns a topic list into finished, captioned, narrated YouTube Shorts — on a schedule, with no human on camera and no paid API keys required for the core path.

Not AI slop. The quality bar here is deliberate: broadcast-grade loudness (−14 LUFS), accurate word-level captions (stable-ts), consistent gold-wipe transitions, and CC0/CC-BY visuals only. Uniform, low-effort output gets demonetized and ignored. This pipeline is engineered to clear the quality bar.

The companion bonus/ folder contains two runnable Python tools (standard library only) you can use immediately: a topic+hook generator and a production checklist generator.

3. Voice that doesn't sound like a robot

The single biggest quality differentiator is the voice. The fallback chain matters because free/self-hosted models are flaky:

Chatterbox (local GPU, most natural)
   ↓ if unavailable
Kokoro (local CPU, fast, decent)
   ↓ if unavailable
edge-tts (free cloud, robotic but reliable)
   ↓ if unavailable
silent (never crash the pipeline)

Loudness normalization

Uploads that are too quiet get down-ranked. Normalize every narration to −14 LUFS (integrated) with a true-peak ceiling of −1 dBTP before mixing. This matches YouTube's recommended loudness and keeps captions audible over B-roll.

4. Captions — the retention lever

On Shorts, most viewers watch muted. Word-level captions are non-negotiable. Use stable-ts (a Whisper wrapper) for timestamp accuracy, burn them in, and style them:

  • Color: gold #F5C518 — high contrast on both light and dark footage.
  • Placement: lower-third safe area, never overlapping the subject's face.
  • Size: large enough to read on a phone in under a second.
  • Timing: word-level (karaoke) sync, not line-level.

5. Visuals you can legally use forever

Copyright claims kill monetization. The pipeline pulls only from:

  • CC0 Public-domain footage (no attribution required).
  • CC-BY Attribution footage (credit in description).

Every clip is logged with its license and source URL so you can prove clearance if a claim is ever filed.

6. Editing rhythm

Shorts retain better with a fast but not frantic cut. The reference engine uses:

  • Shot length: 2.5–4 seconds.
  • Transition: a subtle gold wipe (on-brand, not a jarring flash).
  • Resolution: 1080×1920 (vertical), 30fps.
  • Opening: hook text or the boldest visual in the first 1.5s.
