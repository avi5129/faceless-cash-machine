## B. The 3-link placement that converts

Most faceless creators lose 80% of conversions by hiding the link. Place it in
all three spots on every video:

1. **Pinned comment** — "Full toolkit + the exact pipeline we use 👉 [link]"
2. **Description (first line)** — above the fold, before the `#hashtags`.
3. **End-screen / caption CTA** — spoken + on-screen: "Link in bio."

Do NOT bury it under 200 characters of hashtags. First line of the description
is the highest-click spot on mobile.

## C. Niche selection scorecard (pick before you record)

Score each candidate 1–5 on: (a) CPM, (b) evergreen demand, (c) CC0 footage
availability, (d) your interest. Launch the highest total. The guide's five
niches all score 16+ out of 20. If you want a sixth, run `topic_engine.py
--niche <x>` only after you've scored it.

Rule: **one niche per channel.** Mixed-niche channels confuse the algorithm and
halve retention. Pick one, ship 30 videos, then expand.

## E. Case logic — why this pipeline clears the quality bar

YouTube's downranking targets three things: (1) reused/repetitive audio,
(2) static or stolen visuals, (3) unreadable or absent captions. This pipeline
is built to defeat all three by design:

- Voice fallback (Chatterbox→Kokoro→edge) means audio is never a copy of
  another creator's — it's synthesized per script, with human-like prosody.
- CC0/CC-BY-only visuals mean zero reuse strikes and zero copyright claims.
- Word-level gold captions (#F5C518) maximize muted-view retention, which is
  the dominant Shorts viewing mode.

That combination is why a *local, free* pipeline can still out-rank
big-budget slop: the algorithm rewards retention and policy-cleanliness, not
spend.
